How to find the perfect area for your next house.

5 min read

by homes

One does not simply head out searching for the ideal home without having a must-have checklist! This checklist for buying a house will help you save a lot of time. So, before you spend countless hours checking out property portals and open houses, craft a list of the must-haves you want in your familyhome. And remember, don’t get overboard and leave the nice-to-haves out because it makes meeting expectations tough when you start looking at properties. This tip will not only save your time but also ensure that you are not ruling out too many properties just because they aren’t glorious enough.While looking for a family home, homeowners may struggle with sticking to a budget. They can easily convince themselves to spend those extra thousands to purchase a house they desire — which often hurts their financial wellbeing in the long run. Take a hard look at your finances and get pre-approved before you begin looking for a house. Knowing exactly what you can afford — and sticking to it — will prevent you from drooling over properties that are beyond your means.Look at its Future Prospects Buying a home is not only about going for the one that fits the budget, but it is also about looking into the future and figuring out its prospects. Here, as a buyer, you need to understand that the current outlook of the house doesn’t matter that much as it can be changed over time. Things like flooring, colour schemes, and even the layout of the home can be changed. What matters are the future prospects — the average appreciation rates, neighbourhood growth, and promising projects happening nearby? Try to purchase a property that has room for extensions so that you don’t have to look for another place when your family grows. Look for the possibility of extending the property. For additions, consider the possibilities of a loft conversion, a conservatory, additional space as your family grows. Find out whether the home you are considering has planning permission and if they had made any extensions requests in the past.Before buying, ask for the help of a professional and to complete a building inspection to find any hidden defects. These defects include wet rot, structural damage, and woodworm, only to name a few.Keeping an eye out for defects is important because you don’t want to be making major renovations as soon as you move in. If for some reason you want to do this by yourself, check out some house inspection tips and what should buyers do during a home inspection from the available online resources. A well-built house is a safe haven for your family which means you can plan to stay in it for a long time.Spend some time researching your ideal or preferred neighbourhoods and suburbs. Each suburb has different features, lifestyle and sometimes a unique culture which makes selecting the right location a very important decision. If you’re working professionals looking for a low maintenance home that’s close to public transport and has a selection of restaurants and cafes which compliment your social life, then family-friendly area out in the suburbs may not be ideal. Likewise, if you’re looking for an area that’s close to schools and playgrounds where your family can socialise with other families, city fringe-type areas may not be suitable.So do some homework beforehand and narrow your search to areas that offer the ideal lifestyle you’re looking for.
